About Forest

Forests are vast areas covered primarily with trees, plants, and undergrowth. They play a critical role in maintaining Earth's ecological balance by providing oxygen, regulating climate, and supporting diverse wildlife. Forests are categorized into tropical, temperate, and boreal types, each hosting unique ecosystems and species. About Water Bodies

Water bodies, including oceans, rivers, lakes, and ponds, are vital for sustaining life on Earth. Oceans, which cover over 70% of Earth's surface, regulate the planet's climate and support marine biodiversity. Freshwater bodies like rivers and lakes provide drinking water and habitats for numerous species. These ecosystems are essential for human survival, agriculture, and industry, making their preservation crucial.

About Reptiles

Reptiles are cold-blooded animals covered with scales, including snakes, lizards, turtles, and crocodiles. Found on every continent except Antarctica, they rely on external heat to regulate their body temperature. Reptiles play a vital role in ecosystems as both predators and prey, helping maintain the balance of nature. They inhabit diverse environments, from deserts to rainforests, and include both venomous and non-venomous species.
